Project Trifolium is a self-evolving auto-research system for algorithmic trading under risk governance. A multi-LLM architecture (NVIDIA Nemotron + Anthropic Sonnet) explores strategy design space through an Element Periodic Table decomposition, evaluates candidates with a 9-section D2 framework derived from IMC competition experience, and routes live execution through an institutional Risk Gate. Live deployment used a profit harvester with 12,619 logged Risk Gate decisions and 7 hard-kill layers. During operation, the system discovered constitutional design refinements and produced a natural experiment comparing algorithmic versus discretionary execution, demonstrating that principal-risk is system-risk and that governance must cover both automation and the human operator.
